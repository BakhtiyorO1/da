Samarkand

Samarkand is one of the world's oldest and most beautiful cities, located in southeastern Uzbekistan. With a history of over 2,500 years, it was once a key stop along the famous Silk Road, where traders, scholars, and travelers from many cultures met and exchanged goods and ideas. The city reached its golden age in the 14th century under Amir Temur (Tamerlane), who made Samarkand the capital of his empire. He transformed it into a vibrant center of architecture science, and art. In 2001, Samarkand's historic center was recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, known as "Samarkand - Crossroad of Cultures.' When you're walking through its ancient streets, tasting traditional Uzbek food, or shopping in the local bazaars, Samarkand offers a magical journey into the past with the comfort of modern hospitality. Come explore Samarkand - a city where history lives and legends come to life!
